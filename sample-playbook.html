North Star Statement
By 2027, Customer will use agents to give every resident, tenant, and investor a faster, more responsive experience — resolving maintenance requests without human queues, surfacing investment opportunities before they are publicly listed, and freeing our people to focus on the relationships and decisions that no agent can replace. We will not automate our business. We will amplify the humans who make it exceptional.

The Critical Path
Of the 9 conversations, these 3 are the genuine load-bearing ones right now. Everything else can move if these move. Nothing moves if these don't.
3 Load-bearing boxes
1
Executive pathway
Strategy
Why this is load-bearing
Without a board-level mandate, every other initiative is vulnerable to deprioritisation the moment competing pressures emerge. In a large, capital-intensive organisation, agentic AI will always lose a budget battle unless the board has explicitly approved the investment horizon and risk appetite. Strategy is not the most technically complex box — it is the most politically fragile. If it isn't resolved in Month 1, nothing in Month 2 is safe.
Single most important action
→
Schedule and confirm the board AI strategy briefing within 30 days — with a named executive owner accountable for the materials, the narrative, and the outcome.
2
People pathway
Controls
Why this is load-bearing
In a complex, regulated operating environment — spanning retail, commercial, and residential property — regulatory and liability exposure is acute. Agents taking actions in tenancy management, financial reporting, or customer-facing services without defined encapsulation boundaries creates legal and reputational risk that the organisation is not yet structured to absorb. Controls must be designed before agents are validated — not added after an incident forces the issue.
Single most important action
→
Define and agree the 3-tier autonomy model (supervised / assisted / autonomous) with risk, legal, and compliance within 30 days — before any Wave 1 use case enters the validation environment.
3
Executive pathway
Culture
Why this is load-bearing
A large property organisation has a significant proportion of its workforce in operational, facilities, and customer-facing roles — not digital-native knowledge workers. Change fatigue from prior technology programs is likely real. If the workforce doesn't feel safe experimenting, if middle managers aren't equipped to lead adoption, and if leadership signals are inconsistent, even technically excellent agents will fail in deployment. Culture is not a soft risk. In this context, it is the most operationally consequential one.
Single most important action
→
Run an AI sentiment baseline survey within the first 2 weeks — and have the CHRO personally present the results to the ELT before any adoption program is launched.

The Dependency Map
Which boxes are linked — and what breaks if one is delayed. Critical dependencies in orange. Supporting dependencies in blue.
Cross-pathway links
Read this map as: the box on the left must be in motion before the box on the right can progress. Critical dependencies (orange) will cause direct failure or rework if ignored. Supporting dependencies (blue) will cause delay or quality degradation.
Strategy →
Structure + Culture
Board mandate unlocks operating model change and makes culture investment defensible to the board.
Critical dependency
Controls →
Validation + Production
Autonomy tier model must be defined before any agent enters the POC environment. Controls gate both Validation and Production readiness.
Critical dependency
Use Case →
Roles + Ownership
Wave 1 selection determines which roles are affected and who the business owners need to be. Role redesign and owner assignment cannot proceed without it.
Critical dependency
Use Case →
Validation
Definition of Done and POC environment cannot be built until Wave 1 use cases are selected and scoped. Data readiness assessment follows use case selection.
Critical dependency
Structure →
Controls + Ownership
Governance and decision rights must be clarified before controls frameworks and ownership RACI can be finalised. Structural blockers affect both.
Supporting dependency
Culture →
Roles
Psychological safety baseline informs how role redesign consultations should be run. A low safety score changes the pace and approach to role change communication.
Supporting dependency
Validation →
Production
All three validation gates must be passed before production readiness is assessed. Production checklist is built in parallel but cannot be closed until Validation completes.
Critical dependency
Ownership →
Production
Named owners must be confirmed before any agent enters production. Observability dashboards and SLAs are meaningless without a named owner to act on them.
Critical dependency
Culture →
